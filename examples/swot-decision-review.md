# SWOT Decision Review

## Topic

Should a small team adopt a multi-agent review step before major product decisions?

## Summary

The group should adopt the process for high-impact decisions, but keep it lightweight. The best use is not to replace human judgment; it is to surface assumptions, missing context, and disagreement before the final call.

## SWOT

| Area | Notes |
|---|---|
| Strengths | Makes reasoning visible, records alternatives, creates action items. |
| Weaknesses | Can become performative if the prompt is vague or roles are redundant. |
| Opportunities | Useful for product planning, postmortems, meeting follow-ups, and strategy reviews. |
| Threats | Users may over-trust the output or paste private data into untrusted deployments. |

## Debate Highlights

- The analyst favored adoption because it reduces single-answer bias.
- The executor warned that the workflow must be time-boxed and tied to decisions.
- The user advocate asked for plain-language summaries and clear next steps.

## Recommendation

Use the roundtable only when the decision has meaningful cost, uncertainty, or stakeholder disagreement. For routine tasks, a simple checklist is faster.
