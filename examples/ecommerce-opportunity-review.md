# Ecommerce Opportunity Review

## Topic

Should we explore a generic ecommerce accessory opportunity?

## Context

This is a sanitized example. It does not include platform account data, supplier names, private margins, customer records, keyword tools, or sales promises.

## Roundtable Summary

The opportunity may be worth a lightweight validation pass, but it is not ready for inventory commitment. The strongest next step is to validate demand, compare competitor positioning, estimate landed cost, and identify a clear differentiation angle before sourcing.

## Agent Positions

### Analyst

The category could be attractive if customers have a repeated pain point and current products show review gaps. However, generic accessories can become price-driven quickly.

### Executor

Do not start with a large order. Build a validation checklist: target customer, top alternatives, expected landed cost, packaging constraints, return risk, and support burden.

### Investor

The opportunity needs a clear margin buffer and defensible differentiation. If the product depends only on lower price, the risk is too high.

## Recommended Next Step

Run a validation sprint before committing:

- inspect customer pain points from public reviews
- compare top alternatives by price, feature, and bundle
- estimate landed cost and contribution margin
- confirm whether the product can be differentiated without overcomplicating operations

## Action Items

- [ ] Define the target customer and use case.
- [ ] List five competing products and their positioning.
- [ ] Estimate cost, shipping, fees, and minimum viable margin.
- [ ] Identify one useful differentiation that does not create high defect risk.
- [ ] Decide whether to continue, pause, or reject.
